Concrete Patios & Walkways

Beautiful outdoor living spaces and functional pathways

Transform Your Outdoor Spaces

Concrete patios and walkways extend your living space outdoors while adding functionality and beauty to your property. Whether you envision an entertainment area for gatherings, a quiet retreat, or simply attractive pathways connecting different areas of your yard, Orlando Concrete Driveway Experts can bring your vision to life.

We specialize in both standard and decorative concrete for patios and walkways, offering everything from simple, functional designs to elaborate stamped and colored creations that become focal points of your landscape.

Concrete Patio Options

Standard Patios

Classic broom-finish or smooth-trowel concrete. Durable, affordable, and easy to maintain. Perfect for covered areas or budget-conscious projects.

Stamped Concrete Patios

Decorative patterns mimicking stone, tile, or brick. Unlimited design possibilities that complement any architectural style and landscape design.

Colored Concrete

Integral color or stained finishes in earth tones, grays, or custom colors. Adds visual interest without the cost of full stamping.

Exposed Aggregate

Reveals decorative stones in the concrete mix. Natural, textured appearance with excellent slip resistance and stain concealment.

Multi-Level Patios

Tiered designs that follow your landscape's natural contours. Adds architectural interest and defines different functional areas.

Custom Features

Built-in planters, fire pit surrounds, outdoor kitchen foundations, or seating walls. Integrated elements that enhance functionality.

Walkway Applications

Well-designed walkways improve both safety and aesthetics while protecting your lawn:

  • Front Entry Walkways: Create an inviting entrance from driveway or street to your front door
  • Side Yard Paths: Connect front to back yard with attractive, functional pathways
  • Garden Paths: Navigate through landscaping without damaging plants or creating muddy tracks
  • Pool Deck Connections: Safe, slip-resistant paths connecting pool areas to house or patio
  • Shed or Garage Access: Practical paths to outbuildings and utility areas
  • Backyard Entertainment Routes: Define circulation patterns in outdoor living spaces

Design Considerations

Size & Layout

The right size depends on how you'll use the space. For dining, allow at least 12x12 feet; for entertaining groups, consider 16x20 feet or larger. Walkways should be minimum 3 feet wide, preferably 4 feet for comfortable two-person passage.

Drainage

Proper drainage is critical for patios and walkways. We ensure correct slope away from structures (minimum 1/4 inch per foot) and integrate with your property's overall drainage system.

Integration with Landscape

We work with your existing or planned landscaping, accommodating trees, garden beds, and other features. Curves, borders, and patterns can create visual harmony with your landscape design.

Florida Climate

In Florida's climate, we account for intense sun, heavy rains, and high humidity. We use appropriate concrete mixes, reinforcement, and finishes designed for optimal performance.

Installation Process

1

Design Consultation

We discuss your vision, assess your space, review material options, and create a plan that fits your needs and budget.

2

Site Preparation

Excavation, grading for drainage, removal of vegetation, and protection of surrounding landscape features.

3

Base & Forms

Installation of compacted aggregate base, setting forms to desired shape, and placing reinforcement.

4

Concrete Placement

Professional pouring, screeding, and initial finishing to achieve the correct elevation and slope.

5

Decorative Work

If applicable: stamping, coloring, texturing, or other decorative finishes applied at precisely the right time.

6

Curing & Sealing

Proper curing time followed by professional sealing to protect and enhance the finished surface.

Maintenance Tips

  • Clean regularly with mild detergent and water
  • Reseal every 2-3 years (decorative concrete) or 3-5 years (standard)
  • Address spills promptly to prevent staining
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and deicing salts
  • Keep drainage areas clear of debris
  • Trim vegetation away from edges to prevent damage

Frequently Asked Questions

How thick should a patio be?

Standard residential patios are typically 4 inches thick. If you plan to place heavy items (outdoor kitchens, hot tubs), we may recommend 6 inches with additional reinforcement.

Can you work around existing trees?

Yes! We're experienced at designing around trees, protecting root zones, and creating attractive transitions between concrete and landscaping.

How long before I can use my new patio?

You can walk on it after 24-48 hours and place light furniture after 7 days. Wait the full 28 days before heavy loads or intense use.

Do I need a permit?

Requirements vary by municipality and project scope. We'll determine what's needed and handle all permit applications for you.
