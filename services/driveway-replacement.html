Professional Driveway Replacement in Orlando

Is your driveway showing its age with extensive cracking, settling, or structural damage? Sometimes repair isn't enough, and complete replacement is the best investment. We've replaced hundreds of driveways throughout the Orlando area, working with both concrete and asphalt removal. Our experienced crews work efficiently to minimize disruption to your property and daily routine while delivering exceptional results that will last for decades.

Signs You Need Driveway Replacement

Extensive Cracking

Multiple wide cracks (>1/4 inch), spiderweb cracking throughout, or pieces breaking apart indicate structural failure beyond repair.

Severe Settling

Major height differences, tilting toward the house, or sections that have sunk significantly affect drainage and safety.

Drainage Problems

Water pooling, poor slope causing drainage toward your foundation, or soil erosion underneath the concrete.

Age & Deterioration

Driveways over 25-30 years old showing widespread surface deterioration, spalling, or permanent staining.

Repeated Repairs

If you're constantly patching or repairing the same areas, replacement is more cost-effective long-term.

Major Renovation

Planning exterior improvements? A new driveway dramatically enhances curb appeal and property value.

Replacement vs. Repair: Making the Right Choice

Consider Repair If:

  • Cracks are minimal and less than 1/4 inch wide
  • Surface damage is isolated to small areas
  • The driveway is less than 15 years old
  • No drainage or structural issues exist
  • Overall condition is good with minor cosmetic issues

Consider Replacement If:

  • More than 30% of the surface is damaged
  • The driveway is over 25 years old
  • Structural or drainage problems exist
  • Settling or heaving has occurred
  • You're making significant property improvements
  • Repair costs approach 50% of replacement cost

Not sure which option is best? We provide honest assessments and will recommend repair if it's truly the better option for your situation and budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long will I be without a driveway?

Most replacements take 4-6 days total. Removal and prep typically take 1-2 days, then installation and initial curing take 2-3 days. Your new driveway will be drivable after 7 days.

What about my garage access?

We work with you to schedule the project when it's least disruptive. In most cases, you'll need to park on the street for about a week. We can sometimes phase the work to maintain partial access.

Can you match my existing concrete?

If you're replacing only part of your driveway (like an apron), we'll discuss options to blend the new section. However, exact matching is difficult as concrete weathers over time. Often a full replacement looks better.

What happens to the old concrete?

We haul away all debris and dispose of it properly at licensed facilities. Concrete can often be recycled and reused as aggregate base material.
