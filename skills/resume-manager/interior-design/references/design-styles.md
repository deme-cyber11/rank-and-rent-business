# Design Styles Reference

Use this vocabulary when analyzing Pinterest inspirations and describing the design direction.

## Primary Style Categories

### Modern/Contemporary
**Markers**: Clean lines, minimal ornamentation, neutral palette with bold accents, open floor plans, mixed materials (glass, metal, wood)
**Search terms**: modern, contemporary, sleek, streamlined

### Mid-Century Modern
**Markers**: Organic curves, tapered legs, warm woods (walnut, teak), statement lighting, retro color pops (mustard, olive, burnt orange)
**Search terms**: mid-century, MCM, retro modern, 1960s inspired

### Minimalist
**Markers**: "Less is more," monochromatic palette, hidden storage, intentional negative space, high-quality basics
**Search terms**: minimalist, Scandinavian minimal, Japanese minimalism, essential

### Scandinavian
**Markers**: Light woods (oak, birch), whites and soft grays, hygge comfort, functional beauty, natural light emphasis
**Search terms**: Scandinavian, Nordic, hygge, Swedish design

### Industrial
**Markers**: Exposed brick/pipes, metal and reclaimed wood, factory lighting, raw/unfinished surfaces, neutral dark palette
**Search terms**: industrial, loft style, urban industrial, warehouse

### Bohemian/Boho
**Markers**: Layered textiles, global patterns, plants everywhere, collected/traveled feel, warm earthy tones with jewel accents
**Search terms**: boho, bohemian, eclectic, global, maximalist boho

### Traditional
**Markers**: Symmetry, classic furniture silhouettes, rich woods, formal arrangements, decorative moldings
**Search terms**: traditional, classic, timeless, formal

### Transitional
**Markers**: Blend of traditional and contemporary, neutral foundation, comfortable elegance, subtle textures
**Search terms**: transitional, updated classic, soft modern

### Farmhouse/Modern Farmhouse
**Markers**: Shiplap, barn doors, rustic wood, white/cream palette, vintage-inspired fixtures, comfort-focused
**Search terms**: farmhouse, modern farmhouse, rustic, country modern

### Coastal
**Markers**: Blue and white palette, natural textures (rattan, jute, linen), light and airy, nautical hints without kitsch
**Search terms**: coastal, beach house, nautical, seaside

### Art Deco
**Markers**: Geometric patterns, luxe materials (velvet, brass, marble), bold contrast, glamorous statement pieces
**Search terms**: art deco, Hollywood regency, glam, 1920s inspired

### Japandi
**Markers**: Japanese minimalism meets Scandi warmth, natural materials, wabi-sabi imperfection, muted earth tones
**Search terms**: Japandi, Japanese Scandinavian, wabi-sabi, zen minimal

## Style Modifiers

Combine with primary styles:
- **Warm**: Adds wood tones, textured textiles, amber lighting
- **Cool**: Emphasizes whites, grays, blues, chrome/silver metals
- **Luxe/Glam**: Adds metallic accents, velvet, marble, statement pieces
- **Organic**: Emphasizes curves, natural materials, earthy palette
- **Collected/Curated**: Mix of eras, vintage finds, personal artifacts
- **Moody**: Dark walls, dramatic lighting, rich saturated colors

## Color Palette Descriptors

**Neutrals**: Warm white, cool white, greige, taupe, charcoal, black
**Earth tones**: Terracotta, rust, olive, sage, ochre, sienna
**Jewel tones**: Emerald, sapphire, ruby, amethyst, amber
**Pastels**: Blush, powder blue, mint, lavender, butter
**Bold/Saturated**: Cobalt, crimson, forest green, mustard, burnt orange

## Material/Texture Vocabulary

**Woods**: Walnut (warm brown), oak (golden/blonde), teak (honey), ash (light gray), reclaimed (weathered)
**Metals**: Brass (warm gold), bronze (antique gold), chrome (cool silver), matte black, copper
**Textiles**: Boucle (textured loops), velvet (luxe), linen (casual), leather (rich), chenille (soft)
**Stone**: Marble (luxe), travertine (warm), concrete (industrial), terrazzo (playful)
